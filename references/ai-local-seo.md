# AI Local SEO: Hyperlocal AI-Powered Discovery

## Reference Guide for the attainment-ai-search-visibility Skill

---

## 1. What AI Local SEO Is

AI Local SEO is the practice of optimizing a local business's digital presence so that AI language models and AI-powered search features recommend it when users ask location-specific questions. It is fundamentally different from traditional local SEO in both mechanism and measurement.

### Traditional Local SEO vs. AI Local SEO

**Traditional Local SEO** targets the Google Map Pack: the three business listings that appear in a box above organic results for local queries. It relies on:

- Google Business Profile (GBP) completeness and accuracy
- Proximity of the business to the searcher
- Number and recency of Google reviews
- Local citation consistency (NAP: Name, Address, Phone)
- On-page signals like city name in title tags

The output is a ranked list of three businesses shown in a map interface. The ranking algorithm is largely proximity-driven and review-count-driven.

**AI Local SEO** targets a completely different output. When a user asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, or a voice assistant "what's the best dentist in [city]?" or "find me a plumber near downtown [city]," no map pack appears. Instead, an AI synthesizes information from multiple data sources and generates a prose recommendation. That recommendation may name one business, three businesses, or none specifically. The selection process has nothing to do with proximity in the way Google Maps uses it.

### Why the Distinction Matters

AI systems do not have GPS. They do not know where the user physically is unless the user tells them. When a user types "best Mexican restaurant near me" into ChatGPT, the model either asks for a location or makes an educated guess based on prior conversation context. This means proximity as a ranking factor works completely differently.

Instead, AI systems select local business recommendations based on:

- Frequency of positive mentions across training data and indexed web sources
- Sentiment of review content across multiple platforms
- Consistency of business information across platforms (signals credibility)
- Specificity and richness of service descriptions
- Presence in community forums and discussion threads (Reddit especially)
- Quality of the business's own web content

A business with 400 Google reviews but no Yelp presence, no Reddit mentions, and a thin website may dominate the traditional map pack but be invisible to AI systems. A business with 150 Google reviews, consistent Yelp presence, two Reddit thread mentions in local community forums, and a well-structured website may be frequently recommended by AI.

### The Core Shift

Traditional local SEO optimizes for algorithm inputs. AI Local SEO optimizes for synthesized data richness. The AI is essentially asking: "Based on everything I can find about this business, is it a confident recommendation I can make to a user?" The more data points that confirm quality and relevance, the more likely the recommendation.

## 2. ChatGPT Local Features (2025)

### The Local Map and Knowledge Panel Feature

In 2025, OpenAI integrated local business discovery directly into ChatGPT. When users query local business types, ChatGPT can display an embedded map panel alongside its text recommendations. This feature pulls real-time local data and displays it in a hybrid format: prose recommendation plus visual map.

This is a significant development because it means ChatGPT is now competing directly with Google Maps for local discovery queries, not just providing text alternatives.

### How ChatGPT Generates Local Recommendations

ChatGPT's local recommendation process combines:

**Training data synthesis.** The base model has absorbed billions of web pages including review sites, local directories, news coverage of local businesses, and community discussions. This training data creates a baseline of which businesses are frequently discussed positively in a given city or neighborhood.

**Real-time web browsing.** When browsing is enabled, ChatGPT searches the live web per query. It accesses review platforms, business directories, and community content at the time of the user's query. This means recently indexed content can influence recommendations.

**Knowledge graph integration.** ChatGPT pulls from structured data sources that aggregate business information, similar to how Google's Knowledge Graph works.

### Data Sources ChatGPT Pulls From

The following platforms are known sources for ChatGPT local recommendations:

- **Google Business Profile.** Business name, category, hours, attributes, description, reviews, and Q&A content.
- **Yelp.** Reviews, business description, category tags, photos with captions, attributes (wheelchair accessible, pet-friendly, etc.), and owner responses.
- **TripAdvisor.** For hospitality, tourism, restaurants, and experiences.
- **Reddit.** Community discussions in local subreddits and relevant topical subreddits. Reddit is ChatGPT's most-cited external source.
- **Wikipedia.** For well-known or long-established businesses with Wikipedia entries.
- **Local news articles.** Features, awards coverage, openings, expansions. "Best of [City]" awards lists from local media carry significant weight.
- **Review aggregators.** Healthgrades, Zocdoc, Avvo, Houzz, Angi depending on industry.
- **The business's own website.** About pages, service pages, blog content.

### How Recommendations Are Ranked and Selected

ChatGPT does not have a transparent local ranking algorithm. Based on observable outputs, the selection process appears to weight:

1. **Mention frequency across sources.** Businesses mentioned in more places are more likely to be surfaced.
2. **Sentiment consistency.** Businesses with overwhelmingly positive sentiment across multiple platforms are preferred over businesses with mixed signals.
3. **Specificity of praise.** Reviews and content that describe specific qualities ("their HVAC technician explained the repair clearly and finished in two hours") are more extractable by AI than generic praise.
4. **Category relevance.** Clear, consistent category labeling across platforms ensures the AI correctly associates the business with the query type.
5. **Recency of indexed content.** Fresh reviews and recent web content signal an active, current business.

### Example Query Formats That Trigger Local Recommendations

- "What's the best [service type] in [city]?"
- "Recommend a [business type] near [neighborhood or landmark]"
- "I need a [service] in [city], who should I call?"
- "What are some good [restaurant type] restaurants in [city]?"
- "Find me a [professional] in [city] who specializes in [specific need]"

ChatGPT typically responds with 2-4 business recommendations in prose format, often including a brief rationale for each recommendation. The rationale is synthesized from its data sources and often reflects common themes from reviews.

## 3. Google AI Overviews for Local

### How AI Overviews Handle Local Intent Queries

Google AI Overviews (formerly Search Generative Experience) appear above organic results and map packs for certain queries. For local queries, AI Overviews represent a layer of AI-synthesized content that appears before the user even reaches the traditional map pack.

The presence of an AI Overview for a local query means Google's AI has decided it can add value by synthesizing information rather than simply listing businesses. This is a fundamentally different user experience from the map pack.

### The 35-46% Statistic

Research on Google AI Overviews prevalence in local searches shows that local business queries trigger AI Overviews in approximately 35-46% of cases, depending on the query type and industry. This means a business optimized only for the traditional map pack may be invisible in almost half of the searches that matter most.

The variation by industry is significant. Service-area businesses (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, cleaning) show higher AI Overview rates than pure location-based businesses (restaurants, retail) because service queries tend to have more informational intent mixed with local intent.

### Which Query Types Trigger AI Overviews vs. Traditional Map Pack

**Triggers more likely to produce AI Overviews:**

- "Best [service] in [city]" (evaluative intent, AI adds comparative value)
- "How do I find a good [professional type] in [city]"
- "What should I look for in a [service provider] in [city]"
- "Who are the top [business type] in [city]"
- Questions with qualitative modifiers: "affordable," "reliable," "experienced," "highly rated"

**Triggers more likely to produce traditional map pack:**

- "[business type] near me" (pure proximity query)
- "[business type] + [city name]" (navigation/directory intent)
- Business name + location (branded query)
- Queries with "open now" modifier

**Triggers that produce both (AI Overview + Map Pack):**

- "Best [business type] near me"
- "[service] + [city]" with reviews or rating modifier
- Queries with urgency signals: "emergency," "same day," "24 hour"

### The "Best [Service] in [City]" Query Pattern

This is the highest-value AI local query type. When a user searches "best dentist in Toronto" or "best plumber in Austin," Google's AI Overview synthesizes a recommendation rather than simply listing proximity results. The AI considers:

- Aggregated review sentiment from Google, Yelp, and Healthgrades
- Prominent mentions in local media and blogs
- Website content quality and service specificity
- Schema markup that clearly defines service types and location

To optimize for this pattern:

- Create location-specific service pages that explicitly name the city in service descriptions
- Earn coverage in local "best of" lists and publications
- Maintain consistent, detailed GBP descriptions that include city and neighborhood names
- Build review volume with specific service mentions across Google and Yelp

## 4. Perplexity and Gemini Local

### How Perplexity Handles Local Business Queries

Perplexity approaches local queries as research synthesis tasks. For a query like "best orthodontist in Phoenix," Perplexity:

1. Searches multiple sources simultaneously including review sites, local directories, and community forums
2. Synthesizes findings into a structured response with citations shown inline
3. Presents 3-5 business recommendations with explanatory rationale pulled from reviews and web content

Perplexity's local results are heavily citation-driven. Sources are displayed to the user, which means the underlying data sources are more transparent than with ChatGPT. This makes it easier to reverse-engineer which platforms are feeding recommendations.

Key Perplexity sources for local: Yelp, Google Maps data, Reddit, local news, Healthgrades (healthcare), Avvo (legal), Angi/HomeAdvisor (home services).

Perplexity refreshes its sources with each query, meaning very recent reviews and content updates can influence its recommendations faster than ChatGPT's training-data-heavy approach.

### Google Gemini's Local Recommendations with Maps Integration

Gemini has native Maps integration, giving it the most powerful local recommendation capability of any AI assistant. When a user asks Gemini for a local business recommendation, Gemini can:

- Display an embedded Google Maps panel with pins
- Pull directly from Google Business Profile data in real time
- Surface GBP attributes, hours, and review excerpts
- Link directly to the GBP listing

For Gemini local optimization, GBP is the most critical asset because Gemini pulls directly from it. Gemini's local results most closely resemble an AI-enhanced map pack rather than a pure synthesis recommendation.

Gemini tends to recommend businesses with:

- Complete and accurate GBP profiles with all attributes filled
- High review volume on Google (more weighted here than on other AI platforms)
- Active posting history on GBP
- Service menus or product listings populated
- Consistent business hours and response to reviews

### Bing Copilot Local Results

Bing Copilot integrates Bing Maps for local queries. Its data sources overlap significantly with Google's platforms but also include Microsoft's own local data. Bing Places for Business is the equivalent of GBP and equally important for Copilot visibility.

Copilot local results tend to favor businesses with:

- Active Bing Places listings
- Strong Yelp presence (Bing and Yelp have a long-standing data partnership)
- Facebook Business presence
- Tripadvisor presence (for applicable industries)

### Platform-Specific Differences Summary

| Platform | Primary Data Source | Secondary Sources | Map Integration | Recommendation Style |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT | Yelp, Reddit, web crawl | GBP, TripAdvisor, news | Yes (2025 feature) | Prose with rationale |
| Gemini | GBP (direct API) | Reviews, web content | Native Maps | Card format with map |
| Perplexity | Yelp, local directories, Reddit | News, healthcare directories | No | Cited prose list |
| Copilot | Bing Places, Yelp | Facebook, TripAdvisor | Bing Maps | Prose with source links |

## 5. The LLM Data Synthesis Process

### How LLMs Combine Local Data Sources

When an LLM responds to a local business query, it is not running a database lookup. It is performing a synthesis across everything it knows and can access about businesses in that category and location. The process works as follows:

**Entity identification.** The model identifies the business type (entity class) and location from the query. It then activates knowledge about businesses it has encountered in training data and live search results that match both criteria.

**Cross-source aggregation.** The model combines signals from multiple sources. A business mentioned positively on Yelp, discussed in a Reddit thread, featured in a local news article, and listed with complete GBP information creates a richer data cluster than a business with only one of those touchpoints.

**Confidence scoring.** Based on the volume and consistency of positive signals, the model assigns informal confidence to each potential recommendation. Higher confidence means more likely to be mentioned.

**Synthesis and articulation.** The model generates a response that reflects its synthesized view. The language it uses often mirrors the language it found across its sources, which is why specific keywords in reviews and content are extractable.

### Why Cross-Platform Citation Consistency Is More Important for AI Local

In traditional local SEO, NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) is important primarily to avoid confusing Google's crawler about which business entity owns which address. The consequence of inconsistency is lower map pack rankings.

For AI local, inconsistency creates a deeper problem: conflicting signals reduce AI confidence in the business's data. If a business is listed as "Smith Plumbing" on GBP but "Smith's Plumbing Co." on Yelp and "Smith Plumbing Services" on Bing Places, the AI's entity resolution process may treat these as uncertain matches or partially separate entities. This reduces the cumulative signal strength that would otherwise push the business toward a confident recommendation.

Beyond NAP, AI systems also benefit from consistency in:

- Service descriptions (same core services described in similar language across all platforms)
- Business descriptions (consistent narrative about what makes the business distinctive)
- Specialty and category tags (same primary and secondary categories where applicable)
- Hours (identical across all platforms, updated simultaneously for holiday changes)

### The Role of Review Sentiment Aggregation

AI systems do not simply count stars. They extract sentiment from review text and aggregate it into a qualitative profile of the business. A business with 4.3 stars and 200 reviews may be less recommended than a business with 4.5 stars and 80 reviews if the lower-rated business's reviews contain more specific, extractable quality signals.

AI systems extract sentiment on dimensions including:

- Technical competence: "fixed it right the first time," "clearly knew what they were doing"
- Communication and service: "called ahead," "explained everything," "very responsive"
- Price fairness: "fair price," "transparent quote," "no hidden fees"
- Speed and reliability: "showed up on time," "finished ahead of schedule"
- Outcomes: "my tooth pain is completely gone," "my house is finally comfortable"

The more reviews contain these specific, extractable signals, the richer the AI's synthesized profile of the business.

### Entity Recognition for Local Businesses

AI systems use entity recognition to understand that "Toronto Dental Group," a GBP listing for "Toronto Dental Group," a Yelp page for "Toronto Dental Group," and a Reddit mention of "Toronto Dental Group" all refer to the same real-world entity. This entity resolution is what allows the AI to stack signals from multiple sources.

Entity recognition is aided by:

- Consistent business name spelling across all platforms
- Consistent website URL referenced across listings
- Schema markup that declares the business as a LocalBusiness entity
- Wikipedia entry (for well-established businesses)
- Knowledge Panel presence in Google Search

## 6. Google Business Profile Optimization for AI

### Complete GBP Optimization Checklist for AI Extraction

**Business Name**
- Use exact legal or commonly known business name
- Do not keyword-stuff the business name field (policy violation and reduces entity clarity)
- Ensure name matches exactly across all other platforms

**Primary Category**
- Select the most specific primary category available
- Example: Choose "Pediatric Dentist" not just "Dentist" if applicable
- Primary category is one of the strongest AI category-matching signals

**Secondary Categories**
- Add all applicable secondary categories (up to 10)
- Secondary categories expand the query types for which the AI may consider the business
- Example for a general dentist: Cosmetic Dentist, Dental Implants Periodontist, Emergency Dental Service

**Business Description**
- Write 500-750 character description (GBP allows 750)
- Include primary service types, city/neighborhood served, and 1-2 specific differentiators
- Write in natural prose, not keyword-stuffed lists
- This description is directly extracted by AI systems and often reflected in AI recommendations

**Attributes**
- Complete every applicable attribute section: accessibility, amenities, highlights, offerings, payments, planning
- Attributes are directly machine-readable and fed into AI recommendation systems
- Examples: "Wheelchair accessible entrance," "Online appointments," "Free parking," "No insurance required"

**Q&A Section**
- Populate the Q&A section proactively with your own questions and answers
- Cover: hours and appointment booking, services offered, insurance/payment, what to expect, parking, emergency availability
- Questions and answers are indexed by AI systems and can appear verbatim in AI responses
- Aim for 8-12 Q&A entries covering the most common pre-visit questions

**Posts**
- Publish GBP posts at minimum bi-weekly
- Post types: offers, events, service highlights, team updates
- Include specific service names and city names in post text
- Posts signal to AI that the business is active and currently operating

**Products and Services**
- Add a complete product or service menu with names, descriptions, and prices where applicable
- Service descriptions should be 100-200 words each and describe what the service includes
- This is one of the most underutilized and most valuable AI signal sources on GBP
- AI systems extract service menus to match businesses to specific service queries

**Photos**
- Minimum 20 photos, aiming for 40-plus
- Include: exterior (all angles), interior, team members, work in progress (where applicable), before/after (services that have visible outcomes)
- Photo file names should be descriptive before upload (business-name-city-service-type.jpg) though GBP strips metadata
- Photo captions in Google Photos linked to the business should include service and location context

**Review Response**
- Respond to every review, positive and negative
- Positive review responses: acknowledge the specific service, thank the customer, and reinforce a quality signal ("We're glad the emergency pipe repair went smoothly on a Sunday")
- Negative review responses: acknowledge, offer resolution, move conversation offline
- AI systems read review responses and extract both the business's communication quality and the specific service context mentioned
- Response time matters: responding within 48 hours signals an active, engaged business

## 7. Cross-Platform Citation Consistency for AI

### Beyond Traditional NAP Consistency

Traditional citation building focused on ensuring Name, Address, and Phone matched exactly across directories. AI local requires a broader consistency standard that covers the entire business profile, not just contact information.

AI systems aggregate and compare information across sources. Discrepancies in any of the following dimensions reduce the AI's confidence in the business's data cluster and therefore reduce recommendation likelihood.

### AI-Specific Citation Requirements

**Business Description Consistency**
- The core narrative of what makes this business distinctive should read consistently across GBP, Yelp, Facebook, and the website About page
- This does not mean copying and pasting identical text (duplicate content issues on some platforms)
- It means the same key claims, service specialties, and differentiators appear across all platforms
- Example: If GBP says "specializing in emergency HVAC repair with same-day service in the Greater Seattle area," Yelp should also reference emergency HVAC, same-day service, and Seattle

**Service Description Consistency**
- Services listed on the website should match services listed on GBP, Yelp, Angi, and other applicable directories
- Use consistent service names (do not call it "furnace tune-up" on one platform and "heating system maintenance" on another without cross-referencing)
- Service descriptions can be expanded on the website but should not contradict or omit services listed elsewhere

**Hours Consistency**
- Business hours must be identical across all platforms at all times
- Holiday hours must be updated simultaneously across platforms before the holiday
- Inconsistent hours are one of the most damaging signals for AI recommendation confidence because they create ambiguity about whether the business is currently operating

**Pricing Information**
- Where pricing is published, it should be consistent or clearly explained across platforms
- "Starting at $X" ranges should align
- Wildly different pricing information across platforms signals either outdated data or deceptive practice, both of which suppress AI recommendations

### Priority Citation Platforms for AI (Ranked)

1. **Google Business Profile.** Highest priority. Feeds Gemini directly and contributes to all other AI systems through Google's data dominance.
2. **Yelp.** Second highest. Primary data source for ChatGPT local, Perplexity, and Copilot via Bing/Yelp partnership.
3. **Facebook Business Page.** Major AI data source. Facebook's local business data is accessible to multiple AI crawlers.
4. **Apple Maps.** Feeds Siri local recommendations. Apple Business Connect is the management portal.
5. **Bing Places.** Feeds Copilot directly.
6. **TripAdvisor.** Critical for restaurants, hospitality, tourism, and experience-based businesses.
7. **Foursquare.** Powers local data for many apps and AI systems that license location data.
8. **Reddit.** Not a traditional citation platform but the highest-frequency AI citation source. Presence in local subreddits is a citation in the AI context.
9. **Industry-specific directories.** Healthgrades/Zocdoc (healthcare), Avvo/FindLaw (legal), Angi/HomeAdvisor (home services), Houzz (design/renovation).

## 8. Review Velocity and Sentiment for AI

### How AI Systems Weigh Review Sentiment

AI systems do not use a stars-to-score conversion. They read review text and synthesize qualitative signals. A business with 4.2 stars and reviews full of specific, extractable praise signals ("they found the gas leak within 20 minutes of arriving and had it fixed before noon") will be recommended more confidently than a business with 4.6 stars and reviews that say "great service, highly recommend" without specificity.

AI systems favor reviews that:

- Name specific services or procedures ("the crown placement was painless and took less than an hour")
- Describe specific outcomes ("my energy bills dropped 30% after the insulation upgrade")
- Mention staff by name (signals real human experience)
- Reference the business's distinctive qualities (speed, communication, price transparency)
- Come from verified purchasers on multiple platforms (cross-platform review presence)

### The Role of Specific Keywords in Reviews

When real customers use specific terminology in their reviews, AI systems extract that terminology and associate it with the business. This is powerful for two reasons:

1. The business becomes associated with those specific service terms in the AI's entity model
2. When a user queries for those specific services, the business's data cluster is more likely to be activated

This means encouraging customers to describe their specific experience in reviews (without scripting, which violates review platform policies) is an AI optimization strategy. Post-service follow-up communications can invite customers to share their specific experience rather than just asking for "a review."

Examples of specific keywords that help AI association:

- Procedure or service type: "root canal," "emergency furnace repair," "full bathroom renovation"
- Problem solved: "the leak in our basement," "the mold behind the drywall," "my chronic back pain"
- Distinctive business behavior: "called before arriving," "showed the invoice before starting work," "offered a payment plan"

### Responding to Reviews for AI Signal Extraction

Review responses are read by AI systems. This is an underutilized optimization lever. When a business owner responds to a review, that response becomes indexed content associated with the business entity. It can be extracted by AI as evidence of business behavior and service quality.

**For positive reviews:**
- Acknowledge the specific service or procedure mentioned ("Thank you for sharing your experience with the roof replacement")
- Reinforce a distinctive quality claim ("Our goal is always to leave the job site cleaner than we found it, so we're glad that came through")
- Use the customer's name if they included it
- Keep responses concise: 2-4 sentences

**For critical reviews:**
- Acknowledge the concern without admitting fault prematurely
- Offer a resolution pathway (contact information or invitation to call)
- Keep the response professional and solution-focused
- AI systems extract the tone of negative review responses as a proxy for how the business handles problems

### Review Velocity Signals

AI systems, particularly those with real-time web access (Perplexity, ChatGPT with browsing), can detect review velocity patterns. A business receiving consistent new reviews week over week signals an active, currently operating business. A business whose most recent review is 14 months old signals potential closure or inactivity.

For AI local visibility, maintaining a steady review acquisition pace is more valuable than a surge campaign. Aim for consistent new reviews across platforms monthly rather than requesting all reviews at once.

**Review velocity benchmarks by business size:**

- Solo practitioner or single-location small business: 2-4 new reviews per month across platforms
- Mid-size business (5-20 employees): 8-15 new reviews per month
- Multi-location or high-volume business: 20-plus new reviews per month

### Handling Negative Reviews to Minimize AI Impact

A cluster of negative reviews on a specific topic (billing disputes, wait times, communication failures) can be extracted by AI and reflected in recommendations or disqualifications. If AI synthesizes that a dental practice has frequent complaints about billing practices, it may avoid recommending that practice for queries that mention "transparent pricing" or may include a caveat in its recommendation.

Strategies to minimize negative review AI impact:

- Identify recurring themes in negative reviews and address them operationally first
- Respond to all negative reviews with a consistent, professional tone
- When possible, resolve the issue and invite the customer to update their review
- Over time, dilute negative signals with a higher volume of positive, specific reviews

## 9. Local Entity Schema

### LocalBusiness Schema Foundation

Schema markup is machine-readable structured data embedded in a business's website. It directly communicates business information to AI systems and search engines in a format they can parse without inference. For local businesses, LocalBusiness schema is the most important schema type.

**Core LocalBusiness schema properties:**

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Business Name",
  "description": "Clear, specific description of what this business does and who it serves",
  "url": "https://www.businesswebsite.com",
  "telephone": "+15551234567",
  "email": "contact@businesswebsite.com",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "City Name",
    "addressRegion": "State/Province",
    "postalCode": "12345",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": 40.7128,
    "longitude": -74.0060
  },
  "openingHoursSpecification": [
    {
      "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
      "dayOfWeek": ["Monday", "Tuesday", "Wednesday", "Thursday", "Friday"],
      "opens": "08:00",
      "closes": "17:00"
    }
  ],
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "image": "https://www.businesswebsite.com/images/storefront.jpg",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.google.com/maps/place/...",
    "https://www.yelp.com/biz/...",
    "https://www.facebook.com/businesspage"
  ]
}
```

### Business Type Sub-Types

Using the most specific applicable LocalBusiness sub-type improves AI category matching. Key sub-types:

- **Dentist** (under MedicalOrganization)
- **Plumber**, **Electrician**, **HVACBusiness** (under HomeAndConstructionBusiness)
- **Restaurant**, **CafeOrCoffeeShop**, **Bakery** (under FoodEstablishment)
- **LegalService**, **Attorney**, **AccountingService**
- **AutomotiveBusiness**: AutoRepair, AutoDealer
- **MedicalBusiness**: Physician, Optician, Pharmacy

### GeoCoordinates for Precision

Always include GeoCoordinates schema. This allows AI systems to precisely locate the business on a geographic grid without relying on address parsing. It is particularly important for businesses in areas where the street address might be ambiguous or shared.

### openingHoursSpecification for AI

Use openingHoursSpecification (not the simpler openingHours string format) for maximum machine readability. This allows AI systems to answer "are they open on Saturdays?" or "what time do they close on Fridays?" queries directly from schema.

For businesses with special holiday hours, use the validFrom and validThrough properties within openingHoursSpecification to define date-limited hour exceptions.

### areaServed for Service-Area Businesses

For businesses that travel to customers (plumbers, HVAC technicians, home cleaners, landscapers), the areaServed property is critical. It tells AI systems the geographic scope of service, which is how a plumber in Austin who serves Round Rock and Cedar Park appears in searches for those suburbs.

```json
"areaServed": [
  {
    "@type": "City",
    "name": "Austin"
  },
  {
    "@type": "City",
    "name": "Round Rock"
  },
  {
    "@type": "City",
    "name": "Cedar Park"
  }
]
```

### hasOfferCatalog for Services

hasOfferCatalog allows businesses to list specific services as structured data. This is the schema equivalent of the GBP service menu and is a powerful AI extraction point.

```json
"hasOfferCatalog": {
  "@type": "OfferCatalog",
  "name": "HVAC Services",
  "itemListElement": [
    {
      "@type": "Offer",
      "itemOffered": {
        "@type": "Service",
        "name": "Emergency Furnace Repair",
        "description": "24/7 emergency furnace diagnosis and repair for residential homes. Same-day service available in Austin metro."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

### Multi-Location Schema Strategies

For businesses with multiple locations, each location needs its own LocalBusiness schema instance with unique address and GeoCoordinates. Do not use a single schema block for multiple locations.

Create individual location landing pages (not just a locations list page) with location-specific schema for each. Each location page should also have unique content covering local service details, local team members, and local customer testimonials rather than identical text with only the address changed.

## 10. Reddit for Local AI

### Reddit as the Highest-Frequency AI Citation Source

Research on AI citation patterns shows Reddit accounts for approximately 40.1% of citations in AI-generated responses, making it the most frequently cited external source. For local business queries, local subreddits are among the most valuable AI data sources available.

When ChatGPT or Perplexity searches for local business recommendations, they actively index Reddit threads because Reddit contains authentic community voices discussing real experiences. An AI reasoning about which plumber to recommend in Nashville will weight a Reddit thread from r/nashville where 12 people endorsed a specific company over a Yelp page that may contain review gating.

### Local Subreddits as AI Citation Sources

Nearly every city and many neighborhoods have active subreddits:

- r/[cityname]: Toronto, Austin, Seattle, Denver, etc.
- r/[cityname]social, r/[cityname]eats, r/[cityname]beer
- r/[regionname]: PNW, Triangle, BayArea

These subreddits frequently host threads like "best dentist recommendations," "who do you use for HVAC?" and "looking for a reliable plumber." These threads become evergreen AI citation sources because they aggregate community consensus.

### Building Legitimate Presence in Local Community Subreddits

Legitimate presence means genuine community participation, not promotional posting. Reddit communities enforce self-promotion rules aggressively and ban businesses that post promotional content without community value.

Legitimate strategies:

**Be findable by satisfied customers.** When customers find your business through community recommendation and have a good experience, they often return to the subreddit to recommend you to others. This organic Reddit mention cycle is the most valuable and least controllable AI citation source.

**Engage with non-promotional community questions.** If your business is a dental practice, participate in r/[cityname] discussions about community events, local issues, and non-dental topics to establish a presence. This is different from promotional posting.

**Monitor and engage.** When your business is mentioned (positively or negatively), respond with a polite, helpful owner response. Many Reddit communities allow business owners to respond to mentions of their business.

**Create genuinely useful posts.** An HVAC company could post "Pre-winter furnace checklist for [City] homeowners" in the local subreddit. This adds value to the community, is not purely promotional, and establishes the business as a helpful local resource.

### The r/[cityname] Strategy

For each city or neighborhood you serve, identify the primary local subreddit and observe the community norms for 2-4 weeks before engaging. Note:

- How businesses interact (or fail to interact) in the community
- Which types of "recommendation request" threads appear regularly
- What language and tone the community uses

Then develop a participation strategy that fits the community norms. A natural, helpful business presence in a local subreddit will earn organic mentions over time, which become AI citations.

### How AI Extracts Local Business Recommendations from Reddit

When an AI encounters a Reddit thread, it reads the thread like a conversation and extracts:

- The original question (the local business need)
- Upvoted answers (the community consensus recommendation)
- The specific language of praise or criticism in comments
- The frequency with which a specific business name appears

If a business is mentioned by name in multiple Reddit comments with upvotes and specific positive context, it enters the AI's data cluster for that business category and city. A business mentioned 6 times across 3 different Reddit threads in r/toronto for "best emergency plumber" has a meaningful AI citation advantage over a competitor with no Reddit presence.

## 11. Industry-Specific AI Local Tactics

### Dental Practices

Dental AI search is heavily query-driven by specific procedures. Patients asking AI for dental recommendations frequently specify the procedure they need ("who's the best dentist for Invisalign in [city]" or "find a cosmetic dentist near [neighborhood]").

Key tactics:

- Create individual service pages for each procedure type (Invisalign, dental implants, veneers, teeth whitening, pediatric dentistry, emergency dentistry)
- Each page should address: what the procedure is, how long it takes, what it costs (range), what recovery is like, and why this practice for this procedure
- Build GBP service listings for each procedure
- Earn reviews that mention specific procedures
- Submit to Healthgrades, Zocdoc, and Vitals with complete profile including procedure specialties
- Local dental subreddits and health subreddits (r/askdentistry) are AI-monitored for recommendation requests

### HVAC and Plumbing

Emergency query optimization is the highest-value focus for these trades. "Emergency HVAC repair in [city]" and "24 hour plumber near me" are queries where AI is increasingly the first touchpoint.

Key tactics:

- Create a dedicated emergency services page with explicit language: "24/7 emergency service," "same-day response," and the cities served
- Use areaServed schema to list all cities and neighborhoods within the service area
- Optimize GBP for "emergency" and "24/7" service attributes
- Build reviews that mention emergency situations specifically: "they came out at 11pm when our furnace died"
- HomeAdvisor, Angi, and Thumbtack presence with complete service area listed
- Create seasonal content: "preparing your HVAC for [city] winters" performs well as an AI-cited resource

### Restaurants

Restaurants compete in the most saturated local AI category. Differentiation through AI requires specificity.

Key tactics:

- Menu schema (hasMenu, MenuItem) with dietary attributes: vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher, nut-free
- Specific cuisine and dish-level descriptions in GBP ("hand-pulled noodles," "wood-fired," "farm-to-table")
- TripAdvisor presence is essential for AI restaurant recommendations
- Target food blogger and local media coverage: AI cites local food press heavily for restaurant recommendations
- Instagram and Google photo optimization: AI recommendation systems for restaurants increasingly consider photo quality signals

### Urgent Care and Healthcare

Healthcare AI queries follow symptom-to-provider and condition-to-specialist patterns.

Key tactics:

- Create condition-specific and symptom-specific content ("treating sprained ankles in [city]," "urgent care for strep throat near [neighborhood]")
- Telehealth availability is an AI-relevant attribute (post-pandemic, many AI recommendations filter by this)
- Insurance acceptance should be listed in schema and GBP attributes (AI frequently surfaces this in response to "accepts [insurance type]" queries)
- Healthgrades optimization is highest priority for healthcare AI citations

### Law Firms

Legal AI queries are practice-area specific and often highly local ("divorce attorney in [city]" or "DUI lawyer near me").

Key tactics:

- Individual attorney profile pages with practice area specialization, bar admissions, and case type experience
- Avvo profile optimization (Avvo ratings are cited by AI for legal queries)
- Practice-area landing pages with local context (mentioning local court systems, local regulations relevant to the practice area)
- Legal-specific schema using LegalService type with practiceDomain property
- Attorney bio content that reads as authoritative in plain language (AI extracts and reflects attorney expertise signals)

## 12. Voice + Local AI

### Near Me Voice Queries Through AI Assistants

Voice queries for local businesses are processed through AI assistant layers before reaching any directory or map service. When someone asks Siri, Alexa, or Google Assistant for a local business recommendation, the assistant uses its AI backend to generate a response, which may or may not involve surfacing the traditional map pack.

The key distinction: voice queries tend to be conversational and intent-specific ("find me a Thai restaurant that's open for lunch right now"), while typed queries tend to be briefer ("Thai restaurant near me"). This means voice-driven local AI queries are often more specific and the business that best matches all specified attributes wins.

### Siri Local Recommendations

Siri's local recommendations are fed primarily by:

- Apple Maps (managed through Apple Business Connect)
- Yelp (Apple and Yelp have a long-standing data partnership)
- Foursquare data

For Siri visibility: Apple Business Connect profile completeness is the most important optimization. This includes business description, category, hours, photos, and all available attributes. Yelp profile completeness and review volume are second priority.

### Alexa Local Results

Alexa's local recommendations are fed by:

- Yext (Alexa's local data partner for many categories)
- Yelp
- Bing Local (Microsoft and Amazon have data partnerships)

For Alexa visibility: Yext presence is uniquely important for this platform compared to others. Yelp and Bing Places completeness are supporting priorities.

### Google Assistant Local Results

Google Assistant uses Google's full data stack: GBP, Google Maps reviews, and Google's Knowledge Graph. It is the AI assistant most closely aligned with traditional local SEO signals because of this Google ecosystem dependence.

Optimizing GBP for traditional local SEO remains the primary optimization for Google Assistant visibility.

### The "Open Now" Modifier and How AI Handles It

"Open now" is one of the most common modifiers in voice local queries. When a user says "find me a plumber that's open right now," AI assistants filter recommendations based on current operating hours.

This makes real-time hours accuracy a critical factor. Businesses that do not update GBP holiday hours, have inconsistent hours across platforms, or do not list 24/7 availability when they offer it lose significant "open now" query visibility.

For 24/7 service businesses: explicitly mark 24/7 availability in GBP special hours and add it to schema using openingHoursSpecification covering all days, 00:00-23:59.

### Emergency-Intent Queries and AI Response Patterns

Emergency queries (burst pipe, gas smell, chest pain, locked out of car) follow a specific AI response pattern: the AI prioritizes speed, availability, and trust signals over review volume. An emergency-intent query is where business survival instinct kicks in and the user needs fast, reliable help.

AI systems responding to emergency local queries tend to:

- Filter heavily for businesses that explicitly advertise emergency or 24/7 availability
- Surface businesses with high review volume for emergency services specifically
- Prefer businesses with phone numbers prominently listed (immediate action path)
- Cite sources that discuss emergency experiences positively

For emergency-intent optimization: emergency service availability should be declared in GBP business description, GBP posts, schema description, and website homepage. Do not bury emergency availability on a service sub-page.

## 13. Measurement

### How to Track AI Local Mentions Manually

AI local visibility cannot yet be tracked through traditional rank-tracking tools. It requires a manual auditing approach.

**Monthly Golden Prompt Audit for Local Queries**

Develop a set of 10-15 Golden Prompts specific to your business type and service area. These are the queries your ideal customer is most likely to ask when seeking your services through AI.

Examples for a Toronto HVAC company:

- "Who is the best HVAC company in Toronto?"
- "Find me a reliable furnace repair service in the North York area"
- "I need emergency air conditioning repair in Scarborough tonight"
- "What HVAC company do locals recommend in Toronto?"
- "Best-rated heating and cooling company for older homes in Toronto"

Run each prompt through ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini monthly. Record whether the business is mentioned, what position in the response it appears, and what language is used to describe it.

**Tracking Template**

| Date | Platform | Query | Business Mentioned (Y/N) | Position | Language Used |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Date] | ChatGPT | "Best HVAC in Toronto" | Y | 1st | "known for emergency response" |
| [Date] | Perplexity | "Emergency furnace repair Toronto" | N | N/A | N/A |

Run this audit monthly. Track trends over 3-6 month periods to identify which platforms are improving or declining.

### Traditional Tools Plus Manual AI Auditing

For the traditional local SEO layer that feeds AI:

- **Birdeye:** Review management, sentiment tracking, multi-platform monitoring
- **BrightLocal:** Citation auditing, local rank tracking, GBP audit reports
- **Whitespark:** Citation building, local rank tracking, Google Business Profile management

These tools track inputs (citations, reviews, GBP health). The manual AI audit tracks outputs (actual AI recommendation presence). Both are needed for a complete measurement picture.

### KPIs for AI Local Visibility

**Presence Rate:** Percentage of Golden Prompts where business is mentioned. Target: 40%+ within 6 months of full optimization implementation.

**Position Rate:** When mentioned, what position (first, second, third) in the AI's response. Track the trend toward earlier mentions over time.

**Platform Coverage:** Number of platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot) where the business appears for primary queries. Target: present on at least 2 of 4 major platforms within 6 months.

**Sentiment Language:** The language used when AI recommends the business. Positive sentiment language in AI responses reflects the quality of review content and citation data.

**Review Velocity:** Monthly new reviews across Google, Yelp, and industry-specific platforms. Track this as a leading indicator (inputs drive AI recommendation outputs).
